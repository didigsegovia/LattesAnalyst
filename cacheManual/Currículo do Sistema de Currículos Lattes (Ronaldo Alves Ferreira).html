Ronaldo A. Ferreira is an Associate Professor of Computer Science in the College of Computing (Faculdade de Computação) at UFMS. He received his B.Sc. from UFMS in 1992, his M.S. from the University of Campinas in 1998, and his Ph.D. from Purdue University in 2006, all in Computer Science. Ronaldo was a Visiting Research Scholar and a Visiting Associate Professor at Princeton University from 2014 to 2016. He served as the chair of the Special Interest Group on Computer Networks and Distributed Systems of the Brazilian Computing Society (SBC) from 2011 to 2013. Ronaldo was a member of the board of administration of the Brazilian National Research and Educational Network (RNP) from 2011 to 2013. He has been a member of the board of directors of the Brazilian National Laboratory of Computer Networks (LARC) since 2014. His research interests are in computer networks and distributed systems. 2021 - Atual
SkyNet: Ruma à Era dos Planos de Dados Inteligentes
Descrição: Data plane programability is redesigning how we manage and operate network devices. Recent efforts from industry and academia have shown an increased shift from control plane decisions to data plane-based ones (e.g., routing decisions). Most of these algorithmic decisions executed by data planes are still deterministic and dependent on the control plane. However, we believe that we can break this control-loop dependency and allow data planes to learn by themselves network states and make appropriate choices automatically. In this project, we propose SkyNet, the first effort to implement, operate, and orchestrate Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) in programmable data planes. ANNs will allow, in the near future, the design and operation of domain-specific applications for data planes, allowing them to be trained for a multitude of purposes. Despite the existence of a few initiatives to implement data plane intelligent mechanisms, the design and operation of ANNs are still challenging for two reasons. First, programmable data plane architectures are still quite restricted regarding arithmetic operations (e.g., floating-point operations). Second, data plane virtualization is still in the early stages, making the execution of multiple applications in parallel difficult to realize. To fill in these gaps, SkyNet aims to ease the burden of describing, implementing and operating ANNs for network developers and network infrastructure operators. The project unfolds into four objectives: (i) designing a framework for ANN developing, allowing high-level description and code generation for programmable network infrastructures; (ii) designing a lightweight virtualization environment for programmable data planes; (iii) optimizing the management and operation of ANNs in programmable infrastructures; and (iv) providing a programmable testbed, among the participating institutions, for ANN experimentation. The results obtained in this project will contribute to the research area in the coming years..
Situação: Em andamento; Natureza: Pesquisa.

2019 - Atual
ProgNet: Redes com Plano de Dados Programável
Descrição: The Internet has undergone major changes over the years where new applications and services have emerged with restrictions on network behavior (bandwidth, delay, and packet loss). Quality of Service (QoS) on the Internet has become necessary to provide a smooth operation of applications and services. Unfortunately, QoS is affected by network congestion due to Internet architecture. Network equipment with programmable data plane are the key to maintaining QoS and avoiding network congestion. This project involves the areas of Software-Defined Networking (SDN). SDN logically separates the data plane from the control plane, and enables the processing of flows in programmable mode. The OpenFlow standard is currently the most commonly used solution in SDN. However, the OpenFlow standard uses a limited set of fields and actions, making it difficult to adopt new fields and protocols. In this project, we propose an SDN system (router and controller) to enable the use of new fields and protocols, congestion control policies, and measurement of network performance metrics, without the need to recompile or restart the router when the user changes at run time how the flows are to be processed. In addition, with the proposed system it will be possible to process protocol-independent SDN streams using eBPF statements generated from user-created C or P4 programs. With the new router, we also propose an extension for DCTCP. The prototypes will be implemented on the NetFPGA and Toffino platforms that can be transformed into patents and technology products. Therefore, in this project, we propose to develop the research of a new system with programmable data plan for QoS and congestion control..
Situação: Em andamento; Natureza: Pesquisa.

Integrantes: Ronaldo Alves Ferreira - Integrante / MARCOS AUGUSTO MENEZES VIEIRA - Coordenador / LUIZ FILIPE MENEZES VIEIRA - Integrante / OMPRAKASH GNAWALI - Integrante.

Researching Internet Routing Security in the Wild
Descrição: The project will extend the PEERING research testbed with security-related functionality, including the ability to execute containers on routers, integrating PEERING prefixes with the RPKI (an infrastructure for securing aspects of Internet routing), and making the testbed more reliable. It will also develop algorithms to (1) locate the sources of spoofed attack traffic and to (2) track the adoption of RPKI-based protection against prefix hijacking and identify possible problems in its application. The algorithms will use PEERING?s ability to manipulate routing and its extensions developed in this project to force route changes and observe the impact on the volume of spoofed traffic received from each peer and which vantage points do/do not use routes that violate the RPKI. The algorithms will need to address challenges related to limited visibility of Internet routes, lack of ground truth about routing policies, and lack of control of routing decisions of other networks. The researchers will investigate how the algorithms can systematically change routes in order to narrow the set of feasible explanations to those consistent with all observations, yielding more precise inferences. The project's extensions to the testbed will allow others to conduct novel routing security research, and the algorithms from the project will identify vulnerable networks, a key step forwards in addressing the Internet's lack of authentication for traffic and routing.

Algoritmos e Aplicações para Depuração de Redes Definidas por Software
Descrição: As Redes Definidas por Software (SDNs, do inglês Software-Defined Networks) têm mudado significativamente a maneira como redes são projetadas e gerenciadas. Por ser uma arquitetura aberta e independente de fabricante específico, programadores comuns podem desenvolver as mais variadas aplicações utilizando a API oferecida pelo protocolo OpenFlow. Entretanto, as plataformas SDN atuais, como POX e NOX, oferecem uma interface de baixo nível para manipular diretamente as regras de fluxo dos comutadores, o que torna o desenvolvimento de aplicações um exercício de programação de baixo nível, trabalhoso e bastante suscetível a erros. O objetivo deste projeto foi desenvolver abstrações de alto nível para Pyretic, uma linguagem específica de domínio para programação de rede via OpenFlow desenvolvida na Universidade de Princeton. Além disso, o projeto também desenvolveu um módulo em OCAML para inferência de tipos em linguagens de programação da família Frenetic. Uma nova abstração foi desenvolvida dentro do projeto para introduzir o conceito de tipos nas linguagens da família..
Situação: Concluído; Natureza: Pesquisa.

Algoritmos de Buscas para Sistemas P2P
Descrição: Este projeto consiste no desenvolvimento, análise e implementação de algoritmos distribuídos para buscas em redes Peer-to-Peer (P2P) não estruturadas. Buscas em redes P2P não estruturadas são extremamente desafiadoras devido a maneira como os pares se conectam e a falta de garantias no roteamento de mensagens. As conexões entre pares de uma rede P2P formam um grafo aleatório em que cada par tem um número pequeno de vizinhos. Para se efetuar buscas nessas redes, normalmente, utilizamos algoritmos aleatorizados com decisões locais. A análise de garantias providas por tais algoritmos envolve conceitos avançados de probabilidade e de grafos aleatórios. Nosso projeto visa desenvolver novas soluções para este problema, tomando como ponto de partida uma solução que já desenvolvemos em projetos anteriores. Além disso, espera-se construir, ao final do projeto, um arcabouço (framework) para desenvolvimento de novas aplicações em redes P2P..

Eduroam-br - Education Roaming em Universidades Brasileiras
Descrição: Eduroam (education roaming) é um serviço de acesso sem fio seguro desenvolvido para a comunidade internacional de educação e pesquisa. O Projeto eduroam permite que estudantes, pesquisadores e a equipe de instituições participantes obtenham conectividade à Internet, através de conexão sem fio, dentro de seus campi e quando visitam as instituições parceiras. O eduroam começou na Europa e já está presente em diversas instituições ao redor do mundo. O presente projeto tem como objetivo principal desenvolver um piloto do serviço eduroam em universidades brasileiras, sendo o primeiro esforço para disponibilizar o serviço na América Latina..
